Top AI Legal Assistants: Automating Contracts and Research

Updated: December 22, 2025 • By Mustafa Alhaj Mustafa

Lawyers drown in paperwork. AI is throwing them a lifeline. From reviewing NDAs to finding obscure case law, AI is making legal services faster and more affordable.

1. Harvey AI

Backed by the OpenAI Startup Fund, Harvey is the gold standard for legal AI. It helps lawyers generate insights, draft documents, and perform complex research. It's essentially a junior associate that never sleeps.

2. Casetext CoCounsel

CoCounsel is an AI legal assistant powered by GPT-4. It can review thousands of documents in minutes to find evidence, predict court outcomes, and prepare deposition questions.

3. Spellbook

Spellbook integrates directly into Microsoft Word. It's designed for transactional lawyers, helping them draft language for contracts, spot missing clauses, and negotiate terms faster.

Conclusion

While AI won't replace the judgment of a seasoned attorney, it is automating the drudgery. This allows lawyers to focus on high-value strategy rather than searching for typos.
